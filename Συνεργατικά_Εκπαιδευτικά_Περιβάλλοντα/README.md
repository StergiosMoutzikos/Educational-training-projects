## Project Overview

This paper was submitted for the **Collaborative Educational Environments** course at the **Department of Informatics, Ionian University**. It presents a detailed didactic scenario for a year-long international collaboration between two music schools — one in **Corfu, Greece** and one in **Vienna, Austria** — targeting students in grades 8–9 (B' and C' Gymnasium).

The program integrates two collaborative digital platforms to support project management and musical creation, combining **intercultural education**, **collaborative learning**, and **digital literacy** into a single cohesive experience. The working language of the program is **English**.

## Tools Analyzed

###  Trello
A Kanban-based project management platform adapted for educational use.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Core Structure** | Boards → Lists → Cards |
| **Real-time Collaboration** | Multiple users editing simultaneously |
| **Notifications** | Deadline reminders and activity updates |
| **Integrations** | Google Drive, Kahoot, Quizizz and more |
| **Accessibility** | Web, iOS, Android, Windows, macOS |
| **Cost** | Free tier + paid plans |

**Educational Use in This Project:** Students use Trello to organize research tasks, track progress across phases, assign roles within mixed-group teams, and share information between the two schools.

---

###  Flat.io
A real-time collaborative music notation platform.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Core Function** | Create, edit and share music scores |
| **Real-time Collaboration** | Students and teachers work on scores simultaneously |
| **Multimedia Support** | Embed audio and multimedia in scores |
| **Multilingual** | Supports multiple languages |
| **Accessibility** | Inclusive design for students with disabilities |
| **Interactivity** | Playback and real-time feedback on compositions |

**Educational Use in This Project:** Students use Flat.io to learn and create scores from both musical traditions — Austrian works (e.g., waltzes) for the Corfu school and Greek works (e.g., Ionian kantades) for the Vienna school.

## Teaching Proposal

### Goals
- Understand the musical history and traditions of Corfu and Vienna
- Develop musical performance skills in cross-cultural works
- Strengthen intercultural understanding and respect
- Improve collaborative and communication skills
- Build digital literacy through Trello and Flat.io
- Foster creativity through original musical compositions

### Pedagogical Approach

| Approach | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Collaborative Learning** | Mixed-culture student groups share ideas and resources |
| **Intercultural Education** | Emphasis on understanding and respecting cultural differences |
| **Experiential Learning** | Active participation in performance and composition |
| **Digital Support** | Tools integrated throughout all project phases |
| **Differentiated Instruction** | Activities adapted to individual abilities and interests |

## Scenario Phases

### Phase 1 — Exploring Musical History *(2 months)*
- Form mixed student groups from both schools
- Research composers, traditional instruments, and musical events of each location
- Use **Trello** to organize and exchange findings
- Hold online meetings for idea exchange and discussion

### Phase 2 — Learning Traditional Music *(3 months)*
- Corfu school learns an **Austrian work** (e.g., a Viennese waltz)
- Vienna school learns a **Greek work** (e.g., an Ionian kantada)
- Use **Flat.io** to create and study the musical scores collaboratively

### Phase 3 — Joint Performance & Presentation *(1.5 months)*
- Final **online joint concert** with students from both schools
- Corfu orchestra performs Austrian works
- Vienna orchestra performs Corfiot works
- Culminating shared performance celebrating the year-long collaboration

## Participant Roles

### Students
- **Researchers** — Investigate musical history using Trello for organization
- **Collaborators** — Work in cross-cultural teams via online meetings
- **Creators** — Compose original works inspired by both traditions using Flat.io

### Teachers
- **Coordinators** — Manage collaboration between the two schools
- **Facilitators** — Encourage active participation and creativity
- **Supporters** — Provide technical and pedagogical guidance
- **Evaluators** — Assess participation, collaboration, and artistic development

---

## Assessment Approach

Assessment is **flexible and supportive**, focused on growth rather than perfection:

- **Participation & Engagement** — Quality and frequency of contributions on Trello and Flat.io
- **Peer Collaboration** — Communication, teamwork, and open-minded exchange with the partner school
- **Artistic Development** — Individual and group progress toward the final concert (not perfection of execution)
- **Progress & Reflection** — Personal growth measured from starting point; learning from mistakes is encouraged

---

## Conclusions

The program demonstrated that collaborative digital tools, when thoughtfully integrated into an intercultural educational scenario, can:

- Foster **collaborative learning** across cultural boundaries
- Develop **digital literacy** alongside musical creativity
- Promote **personal growth** in confidence and intercultural sensitivity
- Culminate in a **shared online concert** as a meaningful, high-stakes authentic assessment

The combination of Trello (for structure and project management) and Flat.io (for musical creation) proved complementary — one tool organizing the process, the other enabling the creative output.
